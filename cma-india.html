CMA exam structure

The Cost & Management Accountancy (CMA) examination in India, offered by the Institute of Cost Accountants of India (ICAI), consists of three stages and is held biannually in June and December. The exams follow a uniform fee structure, regardless of coaching mode. The CMA Foundation, designed for 12th-grade graduates, features a three-hour offline exam with 100 descriptive questions. Both the CMA Intermediate and Final exams are structured similarly, including 100-mark papers with a mix of objective and subjective questions, lasting three hours each. To qualify, candidates need a minimum of 40% in each paper and an aggregate of 50% for both groups. Registration deadlines vary, and specific fees are detailed on ICAI's official website.

CMA CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
The CMA exam comprises two parts, each with 100 multiple-choice questions and two essays. A 360/500 score on each part is needed to pass, with a 50% minimum on MCQs. Thorough preparation and review courses are crucial for success.
Candidates should hold at least a Bachelor's degree. The education requirement can be completed before or within seven years of passing the exam. This means that students who are pursuing their bachelor's degree are eligible to take the CMA exam.
Candidates need to have at least two years of professional experience in the field of accounting or finance. The experience requirement can be completed either before or within seven years of passing the examination.
