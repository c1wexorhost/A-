FAQ

When are the CMA exams conducted?

The exams are held three times a year: January-February, May-June, and September-October.

What is the eligibility for CMA USA?

In order to attend CMA USA exams, students need to successfully complete their graduation.

CMA certification eligibility criteria?

To attain the CMA certification, students must pass all parts of the exam, have 2 years of relevant industry experience, and hold a bachelor's degree from a recognized university.

Why pursue CMA USA?

The auditing and accounting industry is an ideal field for commerce and business graduates. CMA USA provides an edge in job applications in MNCs.

How are the CMA exam scores tabulated?

A cumulative scaled score is assigned between 0 and 500. To pass, candidates need a score of 360.

How is the CMA exam graded?

Candidates need 50% (45 correct answers out of 90 MCQs) with no negative marking. The essay part is graded by awarding marks for each correct step.

How are the CMA exam questions set?

The questions are randomly selected from a database, creating a unique exam for each candidate. Some papers may be slightly easier or tougher but strive to maintain fairness.
