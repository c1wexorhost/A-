FAQ

What is the duration of the EA course?

The Enrolled Agent (EA) course spans 6-7 months, offering a comprehensive curriculum covering individual and business tax law, ethics, and IRS procedures

How is the examination conducted ?

The examination consists of 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), assessing candidates on their knowledge of tax-related subjects.

Is there any eligibility requirement for the EA course?

No prior qualification is mandatory, though a background in undergraduate studies or higher education is preferred

What is the passing percentage for the EA course?

The pass percentage for the EA course is 71%, making it an achievable and accessible qualification.

How does EA certification impact earnings?

Earning an EA certification can boost your income potential by an average of 10%, enhancing your financial prospects in the field of taxation.

What does the Special Enrollment Examination (SEE) cover?

The SEE encompasses individual and business tax law, ethics, and IRS procedures, forming the core content of the EA examination.

How long do candidates have to pass all three parts of the examination?

Candidates have an 18-month window to successfully complete all three parts of the Enrolled Agent examination.

What rights does an Enrolled Agent have?

An Enrolled Agent (EA) possesses unlimited representational rights, authorized to represent taxpayers before all administrative levels of the IRS.

Are there any benefits of becoming an Enrolled Agent in India?

Yes, benefits include competitive earning, a rapidly growing job market, no degree requirement, good standing with the IRS, and a secure job position.
