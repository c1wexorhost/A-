CMA exam structure

As mentioned earlier the CMA exam covers a broad base of topics under Management, accounting, and finance. This is subdivided into two parts. IMA is the parent organization that invigilates the CMA exams. Students can attempt for the exams in 3 different windows during a year at pro metric centres across the globe. Those are Jan- Feb, May-Jun, Sep- Oct. Exam passing score is 360 out of 500 for each part. Exam pass rates of over 40%. It is said that students would need roughly 400 hours to prepare for the exams. The duration of the exam is about 4 hours and there are 100 questions and two essay questions in each part. The essay question includes 8-10 written responses or calculations for tackling business scenarios mentioned in the problem.

CMA CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
The CMA exam comprises two parts, each with 100 multiple-choice questions and two essays. A 360/500 score on each part is needed to pass, with a 50% minimum on MCQs. Thorough preparation and review courses are crucial for success.
Candidates should hold at least a Bachelor's degree. The education requirement can be completed before or within seven years of passing the exam. This means that students who are pursuing their bachelor's degree are eligible to take the CMA exam.
Candidates need to have at least two years of professional experience in the field of accounting or finance. The experience requirement can be completed either before or within seven years of passing the examination.
