DIPLOMA ACCA-IFRS

Dear Asperent ,
IFRS, embraced by over 140 nations, is a global standard for financial reporting, mandated for most or all publicly traded companies. Australia, Hong Kong, and South Africa have already integrated IFRS into their local GAAP. The global trend toward IFRS adoption is reshaping financial reporting worldwide. In the EU, listed companies, associates, and subsidiaries must align with IFRS for consolidated accounts. This universal shift is transforming the landscape of accounting principles, emphasizing the need for harmonized financial reporting standards across diverse global markets

The Diploma in International Financial Reporting (DipIFR)

ACCA realizes the importance of international accounting and financial reporting standards in global business. We were the first international accountancy body to offer globally-available, online and open-learning qualifications on IFRS. ACCA was also the first professional body to examine IFRS as part of our professional qualification in 1996. The DipIFR is designed to develop your knowledge of IFRS. It will give you an understanding of the ideas and principles which underpin them and how they can be applied in the international marketplace. DipIFR also builds on your technical and practical knowledge from any recognized country-specific accountancy qualifications or relevant work experience you may have.


Who’s it for?

For professionals in accounting or auditing, eligibility for the qualification extends to those qualified under national accounting standards. Even if currently unqualified in practice, eligibility remains, contingent upon demonstrating two years of relevant accounting experience with a degree or CertIFR. Alternatively, three years of pertinent accounting experience is accepted. Validation of experience is established through an employer-submitted letter, ensuring a robust and inclusive qualification process for aspiring professionals in the field.

What will I be able to do?

Framework Understanding

Understand ACCA’s DipIFR, explaining the international professional and conceptual framework of financial reporting.

Standards Application

FAQ

What is ACCA-IFRS eligibility?

Eligibility requires a degree and 2 years of accounting experience, or 3 years of relevant experience.

How long is the ACCA-IFRS course?

The duration of the course is 3-6 months, offered through ACCA Global with exams in June and December.

Who is the DipIFR for?

The qualification is for accounting professionals, including those qualified under national accounting standards with relevant experience.

What does DipIFR cover?

The program addresses international financial reporting and includes the first-time application of international financial reporting standards.

What will I learn in DipIFR?

You will understand the structure, application, and disclosure of international financial reporting standards, preparing group financial statements.

How is experience validated?

Experience validation requires employer-submitted letters, ensuring a robust and inclusive qualification process for aspiring professionals.

How often is the exam held?

The DipIFR exam is held twice a year, in June and December, at over 380 ACCA exam centers worldwide.

What is the pass mark?

The pass mark for the DipIFR exam is 50%, and it is assessed through a single written exam lasting 3 hours and 15 minutes.

What are the exam components?

The exam consists of four 25-mark compulsory questions requiring both numerical and narrative answers.

Exam

The exam The DipIFR is assessed by a single written exam. This is held twice a year – in June and December at ACCA’s exam centers. We have over 380 exam centers around the world. The pass mark is 50%. The exam last 3 hours 15 minutes and is made up of four 25-mark compulsory questions requiring both numerical and narrative answers.
